INDUSTRIAL DESIGN PORTFOLIO

Jacob Malaska is a versatile Industrial Designer with a strong focus on hard skills, strategic research and formulating the creative process. He is currently based in Salt Lake City, Utah. Jacob's Work consists of many projects spanning consumer electronics, soft goods, and jewelry, among other things.

There are an infinite number of ways to wield the creative spark in one's life; music, painting, literature - But surely creating the implements that we use in our daily lifes strikes at the core what human experience is all about. Our environment and the tools we wield shape us. Design changes behaviour, and for better or for worse, our philosophy. I'm dedicated to the discovery of things that are honest, usefull & beautiful.

RAIL ANC  

IOT FLAGSHIP EARBUDS

ABSTRACT

Rail Is Skullcandy's flagship earbud, and manifested the evolution of technical expertise within the company. Being capable of noise canceling, first class firmware and app control and full voice control, Rail wasn't just our headliner, it was the company's first smart product. My efforts began with form exploration, but quickly gave way to rigourous fit studies. I introduced a system of integrating dozens of ear scans into early and late 3D modeling, making our design process much more data oriented. As the project continued, transparency and atypical use of light became key aesthetic details to drive the story of intelligence. In-ear, the bud has a low profile, clean appearance - while handling the buds and interacting with the case allows for visual exploration and delight with the products many multi-dimensional surfaces.

SKDY GAMING  

MADE TO PLAY

ABSTRACT

The Gaming Lineup was to consist of three sibling products, but we knew from the get go that all three headphones had to have uncompromisingly great comfort, audio quality and comms. We were deterred by the RGB ridden Alien spaceship inspired headphones flooding the market, and instead looked back at retro gaming console hardware for inspiration (the Playstation and Nintendo consoles of the 90's were classicly modern and timeless designs). Gaming presented a lot of firsts for our small Design Team. Boom microphones, suspension headbands and an alternate camber-yolk architecture were all things we knew we wanted, but had to build and test mid development before we were satisfied. The resulting Design was a suite of products that could be spun in either a futuristic or nostalgic CMF easily. The whole family boasted extra large 50mm drivers and exceptional comfort and the same quality boom mic. The entire left ear cup can be 'bopped' for easy mute functionality, and the higher priced point products sported more customization and connectivity options.

RONIN BIKE  

FUTURE COMMUTER

ABSTRACT

Having spent two years in Japan, I became very well aquainted with the Mamachari - The category of cheap, abundant basic bicycles used by grandmothers and business men alike. the bikes themselves were utilitarian, maybe a bit heavy, but comfortable and affordable. unfortunately these bikes have a short life expectancy in the hot humid country, and their over production and cheap perception fueled a negative cycle of abuse and misuse. After see so many poor rusty bikes chained to railings for eternity, I wanted to Design the antithesis. A Bicycle designed for commuting that would withstand abuse. "Ronin" features an in frame battery, rear mounted motor, tubeless tires, and a sealed drive shaft instead of a chain. Safety features and everything else you would want are all tightly integrated into the sillhouette of a road bike.

RINGS  

Tristen Ikaika

ABSTRACT

In collaboration with Shyft Global and their Client Tristen Ikaika, I was able to build a catalouge of ring designs with a wide variety of motiffs for the Ikaika brand. Designs like those we wanted to achieve were outside of the scope of something like solidworks or AutoCAD. Having made my wife's engagement ring, I understood some of the constraints surrounding casting precious metals. Blender's open-source sculpture interface was a powerful tool to take the gestural shapes and fine details we wanted all the way to prodcution and sale. The first rings were floral rose designs, moving on to Animals and other abstractions, finally ending on several figurative subjects where sculpting things like hands, faces and arms in relief around a circular band proved to be a fun artistic challenge.

Circa 11.01.2020

100  

MINAMALISM, MAXIMISED

ABSTRACT

What if one person was to design everything they used to work together as a collection? 100 is an attempt to design each of the objects most commonly found in the American home. As a student, the objective of this project was three-fold.
1. Design common objects in a way that allows them to work together as a cohesive whole while eliminating redundancy and clutter in the home.
2. Give a commentary on the Global epidemic of overconsumption as well as a compelling and finite alternative.
3. Gain a more comprehensive perspective and skill set as an Industrial Designer - Even regarding the "mundane" items that we all possess.

2017 -2018
